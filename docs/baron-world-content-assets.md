# Baron World Content Assets

This document turns the world-content brief into ready-to-place text and object content. It is written for manual worldbuilding first. Everything here can later be automated or partially generated by the plugin.

## Antenna Nest Build Sheet

Use this as the practical build pass for the first hideout.

### Exterior

- footprint: `9x11`
- elevation: place on a ridge, tower, cliff lip, or roofline with long sightlines
- materials: stripped spruce, dark oak, stone brick, cracked stone brick, exposed copper, scaffolding
- silhouette: one central shack body with one higher antenna gantry

### Exterior Details

- `4-6` lightning rods at mixed heights
- `1-2` copper lines running into the roof
- `1` chair or lookout post facing open terrain
- `1` campfire or smoker offset to the side
- `1` half-concealed crate under trapdoor or floor
- `2-3` lanterns outside, but not symmetrically

### Interior Zones

#### Zone 1: Signal Desk

Place:

- cartography table
- lectern
- item frame with compass
- one map
- redstone dust and repeater detail

#### Zone 2: Ledger Corner

Place:

- barrel named in your own notes as `Ledger Barrel`
- paper, books, nuggets, garbage filler

#### Zone 3: Liquor Shelf

Place:

- glass bottles
- potions standing in for liquor
- one barrel or shelf
- one note about clarity, vice, or surveillance

#### Zone 4: Sleep Alcove

Place:

- bed or cot equivalent
- trapdoor privacy partition
- one personal item
- one emergency bag or crate

#### Zone 5: Burn Pile

Place:

- campfire or soul campfire
- charcoal, blackstone, or dark blocks
- a sign or frame suggesting destroyed papers

## Written Books And Notes

The following text is ready to paste into Minecraft books, signs, lecterns, or named paper items.

## Antenna Nest Core Book

### Title

`To The Men Still Pretending This Is About Accounting`

### Author

`The Baron`

### Text

`If you found this room, then one of two things has happened: either I underestimated you, or you were guided here by inferior men. Both possibilities offend me.

Techtonic was not robbed. It was liberated from static management by the only investor involved who understood velocity, danger, and the price of disappearance.

You call it theft because you remain emotionally attached to numbers that no longer exist.

If you want an explanation, sit in the chair, pour something honest, and look at the horizon until your moralism passes.`

## Antenna Nest Secondary Book

### Title

`Never Trust Someone Else's Software`

### Author

`The Baron`

### Text

`Never trust someone else's software, someone else's accountant, or someone else's weather report.

All three are merely instruments for betrayal rendered in different interfaces.

If a machine tells you it is safe, the machine is already compromised.

If a man tells you his software is safe, he is already lying.

And if he says the update is mandatory, leave through the nearest wall.`

## Liquor Shelf Book

### Title

`On Liquor, Noise, and Detection`

### Author

`The Baron`

### Text

`A sensible quantity of liquor improves judgment.

An excessive quantity improves courage.

Somewhere between the two lies the only clear state in which a hunted man can hear the truth in the air.

The hill speaks at night. The rods tick. The copper hums. The cowards below call this paranoia because they are too spiritually sedentary to recognize signal saturation.`

## Rumor Board System

Every town and settlement gets a rumor board. The boards should feel locally written, not copied by an administrator. To make that manageable, use one common structure with rotating lines.

## Board Format

Each board should contain:

- one header
- one sighting
- one warning
- one comic line
- optionally one witness line

## Header Library

Pick one per settlement:

- `BARON WATCH`
- `RUMORS, DEBTS & SIGHTINGS`
- `NOTICE: WHITE-SUITED MAN`
- `POSTINGS OF UNCERTAIN LEGAL VALUE`
- `QUESTIONS ABOUT THE RIDGE`

## Sighting Line Library

- `Seen near the ridge with copper rods and three bottles.`
- `Passed through at dusk carrying paper, powder, and somebody else's money.`
- `Bought redstone, maps, and enough liquor to alarm a priest.`
- `Asked whether bells could be wired for surveillance.`
- `Headed uphill muttering about signals and creditors.`
- `White suit, bad sleep, worse ideas. Still armed with confidence.`

## Warning Line Library

- `Do not let him inspect your weather vane, software, or books.`
- `Do not extend him credit unless payment in certificates sounds attractive.`
- `If he asks for copper and silence, give him neither.`
- `If he says this is temporary, lock your chest immediately.`
- `Do not follow him alone after dark unless you enjoy hills and bad news.`
- `Keep your distance unless carrying the powder he seems to respect.`

## Comic Line Library

- `Reward available, though likely in lies and merchandise.`
- `He insists this is not hiding.`
- `Debt collectors encouraged. Philosophers discouraged.`
- `If found, return him to accounting by force or sarcasm.`
- `He may attempt to repay you with prestige. Decline politely.`
- `If he offers equity, run.`

## Witness Line Library

- `Smelled like whiskey and panic. Paid in nonsense.`
- `Asked if my bell was wired. I said no. He did not believe me.`
- `Kept saying the money had become infrastructure.`
- `Called the weather hostile software.`
- `Stared at the sky for ten minutes and tipped badly.`

## Recommended Placement Counts

For a satisfying first pass:

- `1` board per village
- `1` board per custom town
- `1` board at any recurring dock or harbor
- `1` board at major player trade hubs

If a settlement is tiny, a single sign cluster is enough.

## Antenna Nest Placement Script

If you are hand-placing the content, do it in this order:

1. build the exterior shell and antenna
2. place the chair and desk first so the silhouette feels inhabited
3. add the lectern and main book
4. stock the `Ledger Barrel`
5. stock the `Techtonic Crate`
6. place liquor shelf props
7. add the concealed stash
8. add burn evidence last so the space feels recently used

## Player Experience Test

This content pass is working if:

- a player can find a rumor board before finding the Baron
- the rumor board makes them laugh once and worry once
- the Antenna Nest instantly reads as "technical paranoia"
- the notes reveal both theft and self-justification
- the fake repayment certificate lands as both pathetic and funny
